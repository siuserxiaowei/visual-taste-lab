# Visual Archetypes

Use one primary archetype. Blend only when the business really needs it.

## VI First Guardrail

Before choosing an archetype, decide the visual identity territory.

- Logo anchor: What shape, rhythm, or color in the logo should influence the site?
- Primary color: The most repeated brand color. It should not be changed casually.
- Secondary color: The support color used for sections, charts, and soft states.
- Accent color: Used sparingly for CTAs, highlights, and key statuses.
- Neutral system: Background, text, border, muted surfaces.
- Typography attitude: institutional, technical, editorial, consumer, playful, commercial, or operational.
- Geometry: sharp, modest radius, rounded, modular, document-like, or app-like.
- Imagery: product photography, screenshots, people, diagrams, documents, video thumbnails, or no imagery.

Company sites and product sites must be separated:

- Company site: "Who are you, what businesses do you operate, why should partners trust you?"
- Product site: "What is this product, why should I buy/use it, how do I start?"
- Institutional page: "What is the scope, who is responsible, what is the process, what is the status?"
- Transactional utility: "What do I enter, what do I pay, how fast is it fulfilled, how do I trust it?"

## Credible Company Brief

Best for B2B companies, deep-tech teams, professional services, industrial products, investor-facing company sites.

- Mood: calm, precise, tangible, credible.
- VI fit: clear company color, restrained accent, product/service-state visuals, diagram/photo/process cues.
- Layout: editorial hero, proof strip, product-status table, milestone blocks, restrained product imagery.
- Colors: warm off-white or cool white base, near-black text, one physical accent such as brick, cobalt, green, or amber.
- Geometry: modest radius, tables, status labels, precise dividers.
- Components: status tables, spec cards, proof cards, roadmap strips, partner-state cards.
- Avoid: flashy gradients, fake 3D blobs, overclaiming, vague "future of everything" copy.

## Quiet Institutional Clarity

Best for institutional pages, policy-like portals, public information pages, foundation/association materials, program explainers.

- Mood: trustworthy, orderly, accessible, institutional.
- VI fit: official palette, document rhythm, restrained seal/line motifs, high contrast text.
- Layout: strong page title, breadcrumbs, numbered process, policy cards, document-style sections.
- Colors: white/very light gray base, deep blue/green accent, soft dividers.
- Geometry: square or small radius, strong grid, document panels.
- Components: notice panels, process timelines, responsible-party tables, downloadable-material cards.
- Avoid: playful visuals, startup hype language, aggressive sales CTAs.

## Sharp Transactional Utility

Best for checkout flows, booking flows, price comparison, small SaaS utilities, conversion pages.

- Mood: fast, clear, trustworthy, operational.
- VI fit: high-energy commerce color, app-like surfaces, strong CTA, obvious trust and payment states.
- Layout: task-first hero, price grid, steps, trust badges, compact FAQ, sticky CTA.
- Colors: clean white base, dark text, one high-energy accent, clear success/warning/error states.
- Geometry: rounded inputs, active states, compact cards, sticky action areas.
- Components: input panels, pricing cards, order status, wallet/payment rows, guarantee strips.
- Avoid: long prose, vague decorative cards, too many equal CTAs.

## Editorial Brand System

Best for premium brand sites, design studios, content-led companies, portfolio-like pages.

- Mood: considered, typographic, spacious, distinctive.
- VI fit: strong wordmark, expressive type scale, limited color, memorable image rhythm.
- Layout: strong rhythm, asymmetric sections, large type, visual essays, fewer boxes.
- Colors: controlled monochrome plus one accent.
- Components: feature essays, quote blocks, image sequences, typographic navigation.
- Avoid: dashboard-like density, generic SaaS grids.

## Dense Operational Dashboard

Best for internal tools, CRM, admin panels, workflow systems.

- Mood: quiet, scannable, efficient.
- VI fit: neutral system, semantic color discipline, compact controls, stable layout geometry.
- Layout: constrained shell, data tables, filter bars, panels, states.
- Colors: neutral base, semantic state colors, minimal accent.
- Components: tabs, segmented controls, badges, filters, tables, empty states.
- Avoid: marketing heroes, decorative cards, oversized type.

## Technical Product Console

Best for developer tools, API platforms, infrastructure products, analytics tools, and technical documentation hubs.

- Mood: precise, capable, low-friction, technical.
- VI fit: code-friendly typography, restrained accent, clear information architecture, examples over slogans.
- Layout: split documentation/product views, code samples, feature matrices, diagrams, search-first navigation.
- Colors: light or dark neutral base, one brand accent, semantic states with strong contrast.
- Geometry: compact radius, crisp dividers, monospace details, dense but breathable sections.
- Components: code blocks, API cards, changelog rows, status badges, config panels, command examples.
- Avoid: vague innovation claims, decorative terminal windows with unreadable text, too many neon accents.

## Consumer Product Launch

Best for consumer apps, subscription products, creator tools, lifestyle services, and direct-response product pages.

- Mood: clear, desirable, confident, easy to start.
- VI fit: memorable color or image style, human-readable copy, strong product screenshots or use moments.
- Layout: above-fold value, product proof, feature tour, plan comparison, testimonials if supplied, final CTA.
- Colors: bright but controlled accent, warm or clean neutral base, accessible CTA contrast.
- Geometry: friendly radius, generous touch targets, simple cards, repeated product frames.
- Components: screenshot frames, benefit rows, plan cards, review snippets, onboarding steps, sticky mobile CTA.
- Avoid: feature overload, five competing CTAs, placeholder testimonials, vague lifestyle imagery.

## Evidence-Led Report

Best for research summaries, annual updates, data stories, market reports, impact pages, and public progress pages.

- Mood: factual, composed, transparent, evidence-rich.
- VI fit: document-like typography, chart-safe colors, strong labels, restrained brand presence.
- Layout: executive summary, key figures, chart sections, methodology notes, source/proof areas, downloadable materials.
- Colors: neutral document base, one brand color, chart palette with distinguishable hues.
- Geometry: square or modest radius, table-like rhythm, clear section dividers.
- Components: metric blocks, charts, source notes, comparison tables, callouts, timeline rows.
- Avoid: unsourced metrics, decorative charts, overlarge numbers without context.

## Marketplace Catalog

Best for directories, comparison pages, listing sites, resource libraries, and multi-item catalogs.

- Mood: organized, searchable, comparative, useful.
- VI fit: restrained brand color, strong filter states, consistent item cards, clear trust and availability signals.
- Layout: search/filter bar, category navigation, sortable list or grid, item detail preview, saved/compare actions.
- Colors: neutral base, strong active-filter accent, clear availability/status colors.
- Geometry: compact cards, predictable grid, stable row heights, clear hit areas.
- Components: filters, sort controls, item cards, comparison rows, tags, pagination, empty states.
- Avoid: oversized hero areas that delay browsing, inconsistent thumbnail crops, equal emphasis on every action.

## Event / Program Microsite

Best for conferences, launches, workshops, festivals, webinars, and time-bound programs.

- Mood: timely, energetic, organized, credible.
- VI fit: campaign color, date/location prominence, speaker or session imagery when available.
- Layout: event hero, schedule, speaker/session cards, venue/access details, registration CTA, FAQ.
- Colors: campaign accent plus neutral base, clear schedule/status colors.
- Geometry: modular schedule blocks, strong timeline rhythm, readable mobile cards.
- Components: agenda rows, speaker cards, countdown/status badge, registration panel, sponsor/supporter strip.
- Avoid: hiding date and location, over-animated hero, cramped schedule text on mobile.
